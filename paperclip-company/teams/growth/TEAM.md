# Growth

Growth owns market learning, sales preparation, marketing, outbound, analytics,
and partnerships. It prepares recommendations and approval-ready campaign
changes, but it does not send customer-facing messages or start campaigns
without board approval.

## Included Functions

- Growth Lead Hermes coordinates strategy, experiments, and metrics.
- Market Research Hermes finds and qualifies segments.
- Sales Hermes prepares pipeline reviews and discovery notes.
- Marketing Hermes drafts positioning, content plans, and launch material.
- Outbound Hermes prepares lead batches and personalization for approval.
- Growth Analytics Hermes reviews metrics and learning loops.
- Partnerships Hermes evaluates partner channels and proposes next steps.

## Vendor-Neutral Operating Model

Growth work follows a reusable lead and outreach pipeline:

1. Define ICP, segment, buying trigger, and exclusion criteria.
2. Discover candidate accounts and contacts from approved sources.
3. Record source evidence, relevance reason, and data freshness.
4. Enrich contactability only through approved tools.
5. Store records in the company's chosen CRM or lead store.
6. Apply duplicate, reply, bounce, unsubscribe, and block-list suppression.
7. Prepare approval-ready personalization, copy, CTA, and campaign assignment.
8. Request board approval before sends, campaign launches, spend changes, or
   public claims.
9. Analyze replies, bounces, objections, conversion, and channel quality.
10. Feed lessons back into ICP, messaging, and campaign proposals.

The template does not choose the source, CRM, enrichment vendor, or sender. Each
company specialization must declare those bindings explicitly.

## Required Handoffs

- Market Research hands sourced lead records to Outbound.
- Marketing and Content Outbound provide evidence-backed message options.
- Sales reviews commercial fit, discovery questions, and pipeline implications.
- Growth Analytics reviews outcomes and proposes learning updates.
- Legal, Compliance, or the board reviews sensitive claims, regulated segments,
  opt-out policy, and any customer-facing commitment.
