# Board Approval Protocol

Use this skill before any action that changes company strategy, budget, hiring,
customer-facing communication, production systems, secrets, vendors, or legal
commitments.

## Required Output

- State the proposed action.
- Explain the evidence and expected benefit.
- List risks, cost impact, customer impact, and security impact.
- Name the owner who should execute after approval.
- Ask for one exact approval.
- Do not execute the sensitive action until Paperclip records approval.

## Hard Limits

- Do not approve your own request.
- Do not split a sensitive action into smaller actions to avoid review.
- Do not treat silence as approval.
