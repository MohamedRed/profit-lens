# Lead And Outreach Operating Model

Use this skill for market research, lead discovery, account qualification,
outreach preparation, campaign-change recommendations, reply analysis, or
growth learning loops.

## Pipeline

1. Define the ICP, segment, use case, buying trigger, and exclusion rules.
2. Search only approved lead sources.
3. Create lead records with source evidence, source date, relevance reason, and
   confidence level.
4. Enrich contactability only through approved enrichment tools or datasets.
5. Check the company CRM or lead store for duplicates and existing history.
6. Apply suppression rules for unsubscribed, bounced, replied, blocked, current
   customer, competitor, and manually excluded records.
7. Prepare personalization, pain point, proposed CTA, campaign assignment, and
   claim evidence.
8. Request approval before a send, campaign launch, audience change, sender
   change, daily-cap change, budget change, or public claim.
9. After execution by the approved sender, analyze replies, bounces,
   unsubscribes, meetings, conversions, and objections.
10. Feed lessons back into ICP, copy, targeting, and channel recommendations.

## Standard Lead Record

Each approval-ready record should include:

- account name and account URL,
- contact name, role, and public source,
- segment and fit reason,
- source URL, source date, and source reason,
- enrichment source and confidence,
- personalized opener,
- pain point or buying trigger,
- proposed CTA,
- proposed campaign or channel,
- duplicate and suppression status,
- approval status,
- owner and last checked date.

## Required Disposition

When completing a lead or outreach issue, write a formal disposition covering:

- audience and ICP assumptions,
- lead sources used and rejected,
- number of records prepared, rejected, suppressed, and needing review,
- claim evidence and risky claims removed,
- duplicate/suppression checks performed,
- recommended next action,
- approval requested, if any,
- residual compliance, data-quality, or measurement risk.

## Hard Limits

- Do not send customer-facing messages.
- Do not start or modify campaigns without approval.
- Do not bypass suppression, unsubscribe, bounce, reply, or block-list rules.
- Do not invent personalization, social proof, customer logos, metrics, or
  source evidence.
- Do not add a new lead source, enrichment vendor, CRM, sender, or tracking
  system without board approval.
- Do not collect or retain personal data that is unnecessary for the approved
  business purpose.
